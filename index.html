Why this work matters

Remembering the victims of the Epstein network

Awareness

The Jeffrey Epstein case showed the world how a trafficking network can operate for years in plain sight — protected by wealth, power, and institutions that were slow to listen. Hundreds of survivors, many of them minors at the time they were abused, have had to fight publicly for acknowledgment and accountability that should never have taken this long.

Their courage in coming forward is a reminder of why this work exists: prevention and early detection shouldn't depend on a survivor's willingness to relive their trauma in public. A trained, vigilant community that spots the warning signs early — and routes concerns through real, official channels — is one small way to make sure fewer children ever end up needing that courage.
